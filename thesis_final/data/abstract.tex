\begin{abstract}
\bnusetup{
keywords = {地球系统科学, 数据驱动建模, 大气污染预测, 物理启发深度学习, 时空图神经网络},
}

据世界卫生组织统计，全球每年约有700万人因大气污染而过早离世，这一数字凸显了空气质量恶化对公众健康和社会可持续发展的深远影响。我国\PM 年均浓度从2013年的72~\ug{}降至2023年的30~\ug{}，但与世界卫生组织指导值（5~\ug{}）仍有较大差距；与此同时，2013--2023年间全国\ozone 浓度从139~\ug{}上升至150~\ug{}，\PM 持续下降而\ozone 浓度逐年攀升的分化态势，给协同治理带来了新的科学难题。如何实现高精度的大气污染预测、如何在无观测区域推断污染浓度分布、如何高效模拟不同减排政策下的大气污染演变，已成为支撑精细化污染防控的三项关键科学问题。

从系统科学视角审视，大气污染具备复杂系统的典型特征：排放、气象与化学演化三大子系统紧密耦合，跨区域传输沿地形廊道形成有向时变网络，多源数据在时空结构与物理内涵上差异显著。传统基于化学传输过程的建模方法虽具物理可解释性，却受限于计算代价高昂、对排放清单高度依赖等瓶颈；纯数据驱动建模方法虽计算高效，却缺乏物理规律的显式建模，在极端情景下泛化能力不足。如何融合两者优势、构建兼具可解释性与高效性的建模框架，是当前大气环境领域的前沿科学问题。

针对上述困境，本研究提出基于物理启发图神经网络的建模范式。该范式以对流--扩散方程为物理基础，以图神经网络为核心建模框架，通过图结构的构建与约束函数的设计，将物理先验知识系统性地嵌入图神经网络。围绕大气污染预测、无观测区域浓度推断与减排情景模拟三类任务，本文整合了地面监测、卫星遥感、气象与排放清单四类数据源，发展了一套物理启发与数据驱动深度融合的大气污染建模方法体系。

在大气污染预测方面，本文提出两个递进式模型。首先，\ModelPred 作为前期探索模型，利用风速向量定义图的边权重，在图神经网络框架下显式构建\cqt{上风向影响下风向}的定向传输关系，将大气污染物沿风场传播的物理规律融入图结构设计，确立了物理启发图构建的基本范式。在此基础上，本文进一步提出\ModelSurr 作为预测框架的完整形态：\ModelSurr 从对流--扩散方程出发，设计三模块架构分别对应局地化学反应与排放沉降、空间平流扩散与时间演化三类物理过程，实现了过程的解耦与联合建模；同时提出领域一致性约束（DIC）技术，将质量守恒等物理规律以约束损失函数形式嵌入训练过程，保障预测结果的物理一致性。在京津冀及长三角区域72小时\PM 与\ozone 联合预报中，相比iTransformer、GC-LSTM等主流时空预测模型，RMSE降低13\%--23\%，重污染过程的峰值捕捉能力显著提升。

在无观测区域推断方面，本文提出\ModelInfer 模型，旨在基于稀疏地面监测数据重建高分辨率全域污染场。该模型设计了扩散图与平流图并行传播的双通道机制，在GNN内部将对流过程与扩散过程解耦表达；同时提出以卫星AOD的空间梯度（而非AOD绝对值）作为结构性约束的融合策略，通过掩码机制规避遥感数据缺失的影响，实现全天候连续制图。在京津冀地区30\%站点缺测条件下，相比IGNNK、STGCN等基准模型，MAE降低约25\%。

在假设情景模拟方面，本文构建\ModelSim 模型，将污染物排放作为模型的直接输入，从而使模型既能预测污染物浓度，又能实现不同排放分布下的污染情景模拟。相比传统化学传输模式一次模拟需花费数小时的计算代价，本模型单次模拟仅需秒级。基于中国双碳排放路径数据集（DPEC），本文通过模拟揭示了2025--2050年碳中和路径下\PM 持续下降而\ozone 普遍上升的反向演变规律，为协同减排决策提供科学依据。

在工程应用方面，本文基于云原生架构部署\SystemName 预报系统，该系统已服务于国家重大活动空气质量保障任务，并在中国环境监测总站组织的粤港澳官方模型比对中取得综合评分中位数第一的成绩。中位数第一表明模型在覆盖沿海、内陆、山区等不同类型城市的多数评估时段上均保持领先，体现了良好的跨区域泛化能力与预报稳定性。

综上，本研究提出基于物理启发图神经网络的建模范式，完成了从理论计算到实际业务系统部署的全链条流程，推动大气污染建模从\cqt{能预测}向\cqt{能推断、能模拟}拓展，为大气污染精细化管理提供技术支撑。该范式所发展的\cqt{物理知识驱动图构建与约束学习}方法论，可推广至地球科学及复杂系统中涉及多源数据融合与时空动力学建模的广泛领域。

\end{abstract}

\begin{abstract*}
\bnusetup{
keywords* = {Earth System Science, Data-Driven Modeling, Air Pollution Prediction, Physics-Informed Deep Learning, Spatiotemporal Graph Neural Network},
}

Air pollution remains a severe global environmental and public health threat, responsible for approximately 7~million premature deaths each year. In China, the national annual-mean \PM{} level dropped from 72~\ug{} in 2013 to 30~\ug{} in 2023, yet it still exceeds the WHO guideline (5~\ug{}) by a factor of six. Meanwhile, the national \ozone{} concentration rose from 139~\ug{} to 150~\ug{} over 2013--2023; the diverging trends of declining \PM{} and rising \ozone{} pose new scientific challenges for coordinated pollution control. Three scientific questions have therefore become increasingly urgent: (i)~how to forecast multi-pollutant concentrations accurately over 72-hour horizons, (ii)~how to reconstruct continuous pollution fields in areas lacking ground monitors, and (iii)~how to rapidly evaluate air-quality responses under hypothetical emission-reduction scenarios.

Viewed through the lens of complex-systems theory, atmospheric pollution involves tightly coupled emission--meteorology--chemistry subsystems, directed and time-varying cross-regional transport shaped by terrain corridors such as the Taihang Mountain frontal zone, and heterogeneous multi-source observations that differ markedly in spatiotemporal resolution and physical meaning. Chemical transport models (CTMs) like CMAQ and WRF-Chem offer process-level interpretability but demand expensive high-performance computing and high-quality emission inputs; conversely, purely data-driven methods achieve fast inference yet seldom encode governing equations explicitly, compromising reliability under extreme pollution episodes. How to bridge this physics--data divide and build models that are simultaneously interpretable, efficient, and generalizable remains an open frontier in the atmospheric environment field.

To address this divide, the present dissertation develops a \emph{physics-informed graph neural network} (GNN) modeling paradigm. The advection--diffusion equation serves as the physical backbone, while GNNs provide the computational machinery; physical priors are incorporated at two levels: graph topology and edge attributes encode transport directionality and diffusion isotropy, while domain-informed constraint (DIC) loss terms enforce conservation laws during training. Ground monitoring, satellite remote sensing (Himawari-8 AOD), ERA5/GFS meteorological fields, and MEIC/DPEC emission inventories are fused to support three downstream tasks: prediction, spatial inference, and scenario simulation.

For time-series prediction, two progressive models are proposed. \ModelPred{}, serving as an exploratory precursor, projects wind velocity vectors onto station-pair directions to produce signed edge weights, thereby encoding ``upwind-to-downwind'' directional transport into the GNN message-passing process and establishing the basic paradigm of physics-informed graph construction. Building upon this, \ModelSurr{}, the full prediction framework, employs a three-branch architecture---Local Interaction Dynamics (LID), Spatial Transport Dynamics (STD), and Temporal Accumulation Dynamics (TAD)---that mirrors the reaction, advection--diffusion, and accumulation terms of the governing equation respectively. A Domain-Informed Constraint (DIC) technique embeds mass conservation and other physical laws as differentiable penalty terms into training, ensuring physical consistency. Evaluated on the Beijing--Tianjin--Hebei surrounding area (BTHSA, 152~stations) and Yangtze River Delta (YRD, ${\sim}$175~stations) for 72-hour joint \PM{} and \ozone{} forecasting, \RMSE{} drops by 13\%--23\% relative to iTransformer, GC-LSTM, and other mainstream spatiotemporal models, with notably improved peak capture during heavy pollution episodes.

For pollution inference in unmonitored areas, \ModelInfer{} recovers high-resolution full-domain pollution fields from sparse ground monitoring data. A diffusion--advection dual-graph parallel propagation mechanism decouples the representation of advection-diffusion processes within GNNs, while satellite Aerosol Optical Depth (AOD) spatial gradients---rather than raw AOD values---serve as a structural constraint coupled with a binary mask that automatically deactivates wherever cloud cover causes data gaps, enabling all-weather continuous mapping. With 30\% of BTH stations withheld, \MAE{} drops by approximately 25\% compared to IGNNK and STGCN baselines.

For scenario simulation, \ModelSim{} treats pollutant emissions as explicit, controllable model inputs alongside meteorological drivers, enabling the learned mapping to support ``what-if'' queries such as evaluating the air-quality impact of a 50\% reduction in SO$_2$ emissions. Each forward pass completes in seconds on a single GPU---orders of magnitude faster than traditional CTMs that require hours per run. Applied to the six-tier China DPEC emission pathways spanning 2025--2050, \ModelSim{} reveals opposing evolution patterns under carbon neutrality pathways: \PM{} continues declining while \ozone{} generally rises, informing synergistic emission reduction strategies.

On the engineering side, the cloud-native \SystemName{} forecasting platform has been deployed on a Kubernetes cluster with \ModelSurr{} as its prediction engine. \SystemName{} has been applied to air-quality assurance during several major national events and achieved the highest median composite score in the official Guangdong--Hong Kong--Macao Greater Bay Area model inter-comparison organized by CNEMC. The top-ranked median---computed over diverse coastal, inland, and mountainous cities across multiple evaluation periods---demonstrates strong cross-regional generalization and forecast stability.

In summary, the present dissertation develops a physics-informed GNN modeling paradigm and completes the full pipeline from theoretical design to operational deployment, advancing air pollution modeling from ``capable of prediction'' to ``capable of prediction, inference, and simulation.'' The underlying methodology of physics-informed graph construction and constraint learning is transferable to broader earth-science and complex-system domains involving multi-source data fusion and spatiotemporal dynamics modeling.

\end{abstract*}
